Abstract

As mobile devices have become the primary gateway to the internet, mobile browsers have evolved from simple WAP viewers into complex platforms. This research explores the technical shift from desktop-centric browsing to mobile-first environments, examining how constraints in hardware, battery life, and screen real estate have driven innovation in rendering engines and security protocols.

Core Research Themes

This project focuses on three primary areas of mobile browser technology:

  1. Efficiency: Reducing data usage and CPU cycles.
  2. Interoperability: Ensuring a consistent experience across iOS and Android.
  3. Privacy: Combatting cross-site tracking in a mobile environment.
